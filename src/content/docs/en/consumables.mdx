# Consumables

Your workforce doesn't work for free — **29 consumable types** keep them fed, clothed, healthy, and happy. If any required consumable runs out, that workforce tier's buildings slow down or halt entirely. Supplying your own workforce is often cheaper than buying prefabs off the CX; supplying *other* players' workforces is a stable long-term business.

## Consumable Bundles (5)

Bundles are **pre-packaged** consumable sets made in **PAC** (Packaging) buildings. They're mostly used for player-to-player supply contracts — one COLONY consumption plan = one bundle per day.

| Ticker | For | Recipe |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **PBU** | Pioneer | 50× PE + 5× COF + 40× DW + 40× RAT + 5× OVE + 2× PWO → 1× PBU (4.8h) |
| **SBU** | Settler | 60× RAT + 100× PE + 10× KOM + 50× DW + 5× EXO + 5× PT + 2× REP → 1× SBU (5.3h) |
| **TBU** | Technician | 70× RAT + 1× PSS + 1× SCN + 1× SC + 40× PE + 5× HMS + 75× DW + 5× MED + 10× ALE → 1× TBU (6h) |
| **EBU** | Engineer | 4× PSS + 70× FIM + 1× PDA + 10× GIN + 160× PE + 5× MED + 2× HSS + 100× DW + 2× VG → 1× EBU (6.7h) |
| **CBU** | Scientist | 10× WIN + 280× PE + 5× MED + 2× LC + 1× WS + 100× DW + 70× MEA + 8× PSS + 1× NST → 1× CBU (7.2h) |

> **PE (Polyethylene) is the wrapping.** Every bundle uses 50–280 PE — often the biggest single cost.

## Tips

1. **Basic consumables first.** Missing a luxury cuts efficiency 10–20%. Missing a basic stops production. Always prioritize basics.
2. **Water is universal.** Every tier needs DW. A reliable water supply (either local H2O extraction or imports) is the #1 bottleneck on most colonies.
3. **RAT carries 3 tiers.** Pioneers, Settlers, and Technicians all eat RAT — it's the single most-produced consumable in the game. Build multiple FPs early.
4. **Engineers and Scientists eat FIM/MEA.** These are processed RAT → FIM → MEA. Plan the food chain accordingly before upgrading your workforce.
5. **Luxuries boost efficiency per worker — cheap ones first.** COF is the cheapest luxury for Pioneers; KOM for Settlers. Supply at least one luxury from day 1.
6. **Self-supply vs buy from CX.** Calculate per-day cost: 100 Pioneers eat 4 RAT × 3 tickers CX price + 4 DW × 3 tickers = ~20 CX tickers/day. Compare against growing locally with 2 FRM + 1 FP.
7. **Bundle contracts are stable income.** If you have spare capacity, selling PBU/SBU on long-term contracts is far more stable than chasing CX price swings.
