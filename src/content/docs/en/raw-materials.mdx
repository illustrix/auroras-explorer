# Raw Materials

Everything in Prosperous Universe starts here. Raw materials are extracted directly from planets and moons — **44 total** across ores, gases, minerals, and elements. Pick the wrong planet and your production line starves; pick the right one and you'll outrun competitors.

## Extraction Buildings

Three Pioneer-staffed buildings handle all planetary extraction:

| Building | Name | Workforce | Area | What It Extracts |
|----------|------|-----------|------|------------------|
| **EXT** | Extractor | 60 Pioneer | 25 | Ores, minerals, liquids |
| **COL** | Collector | 50 Pioneer | 15 | Gases only |
| **RIG** | Rig | 30 Pioneer | 10 | Ores, minerals, liquids (smaller/faster) |

> **How extraction works:** Output depends entirely on **what the planet has**. Each planet has a fixed set of resources with specific **concentration** values. Higher concentration = faster extraction. The building has no "recipe" — you place it on a planet and it pulls whatever's there.

**EXT vs RIG:** EXT has higher total throughput but takes more area and workforce. RIG is cheaper to run but slower per unit. For rare/low-concentration resources, RIG is often more cost-efficient; for abundant high-concentration deposits, EXT wins.

## Ores (8 types)

Smelted into metals in **SME** (Smelter) or **AML** (Advanced Material Lab).

| Ticker | Name | Smelts Into | Key Recipe |
|--------|------|-------------|-----------|
| **FEO** | Iron Ore | FE (Iron) | 6× FEO + 1× C + 1× O → 3× FE (12h) |
| **ALO** | Aluminum Ore | AL (Aluminum) | 6× ALO + 1× C + 1× O → 3× AL (12h) |
| **CUO** | Copper Ore | CU (Copper) | 5× CUO + 1× SIO + 10× O → 3× CU (12h) |
| **SIO** | Silicon Ore | SI (Silicon) | 3× SIO + 1× AL → 1× SI (3.6h) |
| **AUO** | Gold Ore | AU (Gold) | 3× AUO + 1× C → 2× AU (10.8h) |
| **LIO** | Lithium Ore | LI (Lithium) | 10× LIO + 4× HAL → 4× LI (15.8h) |
| **TIO** | Titanium Ore | TI (Titanium) | 4× TIO + 1× C + 1× O → 2× TI (18h) |
| **REO** | Rare Earth Ore | RE (Rare Earths) | 8× REO + 1× C + 1× O → 4× RE (19.2h) |

> **FLX (Flux) boosts yield:** Adding 1× FLX to FEO/ALO/REO smelting gives +33% output. FLX comes from LST (Limestone) in a CHP, so factor that cost in.

## Gases (10 types)

Collected with **COL**. Feed every chemical and fuel chain.

| Ticker | Name | Main Uses |
|--------|------|-----------|
| **H** | Hydrogen | Fuels (SF, FF, VF), chemicals (AMM, H2O) |
| **O** | Oxygen | Smelting flux, water, consumables |
| **N** | Nitrogen | Ammonia (AMM), life support |
| **HE** | Helium | Cryogenics, superconductors |
| **HE3** | Helium-3 | FTL fuel (FF), refined from TS |
| **F** | Fluorine | High-end chemicals, rocket fuel |
| **AMM** | Ammonia | Fertilizer, cleaning products |
| **AR** | Argon | Noble gas, shielding |
| **KR** | Krypton | Specialty electronics |
| **NE** | Neon | Lighting, lasers |

**H + HE3 → FF (FTL Fuel):** The entire FTL travel economy runs on this one recipe. HE3 is rare and expensive — often produced from TS (Thorium) in a REF instead of collected directly.

## Minerals (14 types)

Extracted with EXT/RIG. Processed into elements by **CHP**, **AML**, **BMP**, or used directly.

### Common Minerals (CHP / BMP processing)

| Ticker | Name | Processed To |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **LST** | Limestone | CA (Calcium), FLX (Flux) |
| **HAL** | Halite (Salt) | NA (Sodium) + CL (Chlorine) |
| **CLI** | Clay | I (Iodine) |
| **MGS** | Magnesite | MG (Magnesium) |
| **SCR** | Sulfur-bearing rock | S (Sulfur) via SME |
| **TCO** | Technetium Ore | TC (Technetium) via TNP |

### Rare Minerals (AML processing — Tech/Eng workforce)

| Ticker | Name | Processed To |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **BER** | Beryl | BE (Beryllium) + bonus AL + SIO |
| **BOR** | Borax | BOS (Boron Oxide) — needs 4× AL + 1× SI |
| **TAI** | Tantalite | TA (Tantalum) + bonus FE |
| **ZIR** | Zircon | ZR (Zirconium) + bonus SIO |
| **BTS** | Bauxite/Tungsten | W (Tungsten) via AML — 5× BTS → 1× W |

### Liquid/Special

| Ticker | Name | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **TS** | Thorium | Refined to HE3 in REF (3× TS → 2× HE3) |
| **MAG** | Magnetite | Used in construction and electronics |
| **BRM** | Bromine-bearing | Chemical feedstock |

## Elements (12 types)

The "periodic table" materials — basic chemistry building blocks.

| Ticker | Name | Source | Used For |
|--------|------|--------|----------|
| **C** | Carbon | HCP → INC (or GRN/MAI) | Smelting, plastics, almost everything |
| **O** | Oxygen | Collected gas | Smelting flux, water, consumables |
| **H** | Hydrogen | Collected gas | Fuels, water, ammonia |
| **CA** | Calcium | LST → CHP | Concrete, glass, consumables |
| **NA** | Sodium | HAL → CHP | Chemicals, solar panels |
| **CL** | Chlorine | HAL → CHP | Disinfectants, plastics |
| **MG** | Magnesium | MGS → BMP | Alloys, electronics |
| **I** | Iodine | CLI → BMP | Medical, chemicals |
| **S** | Sulfur | SCR → SME | Sulfuric acid, rubber |
| **BE** | Beryllium | BER → AML | Advanced alloys, electronics |
| **TA** | Tantalum | TAI → AML | Capacitors, high-end electronics |
| **ZR** | Zirconium | ZIR → AML | Heat-resistant alloys |
| **TC** | Technetium | TCO → TNP | Radioisotopes, advanced chemistry |

**HCP is the carbon backbone:** Carbon (C) is consumed everywhere — smelting, plastics, chemicals. Most players grow HCP on a farm and burn it in an Incinerator (INC) rather than importing C.

## Fuels (3 types) — Refinery (REF)

| Ticker | Name | Recipe | Used For |
|--------|------|--------|----------|
| **SF** | STL Fuel | 2× GAL + 1× AMM + 3× H → 100× SF (3.6h) | Sublight ship movement (in-system) |
| **FF** | FTL Fuel | 4× H + 2× HE3 → 100× FF (3.6h) | FTL jumps (between systems) |
| **VF** | Vessel Fuel | 4× H + 2× KRE → 2000× VF (12h) | Specialty vessels |

> **Alt SF recipe:** 1× AMM + 5× NAB → 150× SF (9.6h). NAB is a nitrogen-rich chemical — sometimes cheaper than GAL depending on your market.

## Tips for Picking Extraction Planets

1. **Check concentration first.** A planet with 0.5 concentration FEO produces 5× more than one at 0.1. Always compare before settling.
2. **Gas giants are gas-only.** If you need ores and gases, you need two planets — or ship between them.
3. **Fertility matters for farms, not for EXT.** Rocky barren planets with rich ores beat fertile ones for mining bases.
4. **Water (H2O) is a liquid resource.** Extracted with EXT/RIG, not from ice. Some planets have zero water — plan supply lines.
5. **AML buildings need Tech+Eng workforce.** Rare-element mining requires a mature base with full workforce tiers — not a starting-planet activity.
6. **HE3 is usually cheaper refined than collected.** Check the math: 3× TS → 2× HE3 via REF often beats raw HE3 collection costs.
7. **Don't neglect C.** Carbon shortage bottlenecks smelting. Most mature bases grow HCP locally rather than importing C.
