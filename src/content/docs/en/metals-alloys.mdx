# Metals & Alloys

If raw materials are the foundation, metals and alloys are the skeleton. **10 metals + 10 alloys** feed almost every downstream product — from basic hulls to endgame ship engines. This chain is dominated by a single building type (**SME** / **ASM**), making it one of the most **area-efficient** production lines you can run.

## Processing Buildings

| Building | Name | Workforce | Area | Handles |
|----------|------|-----------|------|---------|
| **SME** | Smelter | 50 Pioneer | 17 | Basic metal smelting (Ore → Metal) |
| **ASM** | Advanced Smelter | 50 Tech + 20 Eng | 34 | Most alloys |
| **FS** | Fine Smithy | 50 Settler | 25 | Gold alloys (BGO, RGO, BRO) |
| **AML** | Advanced Material Lab | 20 Tech + 30 Eng | 45 | Rare metals (BE, W, TA, ZR) and BOS |

## Basic Metals (10)

All 10 metals come from SME (except **W** which needs AML):

| Ticker | Name | Primary Recipe | Time | FLX Variant |
|--------|------|---------------|------|-------------|
| **FE** | Iron | 6× FEO + 1× C + 1× O → 3× FE | 12h | +FLX → 4× FE (14.4h) |
| **AL** | Aluminum | 6× ALO + 1× C + 1× O → 3× AL | 12h | +FLX → 4× AL (14.4h) |
| **CU** | Copper | 5× CUO + 1× SIO + 10× O → 3× CU | 12h | — |
| **AU** | Gold | 3× AUO + 1× C → 2× AU | 10.8h | — |
| **SI** | Silicon | 3× SIO + 1× AL → 1× SI | 3.6h | alt: 3× SIO + 1× FLX + 1× O + 1× C → 1× SI (2.9h) |
| **LI** | Lithium | 10× LIO + 4× HAL → 4× LI | 15.8h | — |
| **TI** | Titanium | 4× TIO + 1× C + 1× O → 2× TI | 18h | — |
| **RE** | Rare Earth | 8× REO + 1× C + 1× O → 4× RE | 19.2h | +FLX → 5× RE (19.2h) |
| **STL** | Steel | 2× FE + 8× O → 2× STL | 8.4h | — (FE is already smelted) |
| **W** | Tungsten | 5× BTS → 1× W (in AML) | 14.4h | rare mineral path |

> **FLX is almost always worth it** for FE, AL, and REO smelting — it turns 3× output into 4–5×. FLX comes from LST in a CHP. A single CHP producing FLX can feed a whole row of smelters.

| Metal | Used in # Recipes | Primary Role |
|-------|-------------------|--------------|
| **AL** | 41 | Universal — alloys, electronics, construction, everything |
| **FE** | 15 | Steel, construction, alloys, basic parts |
| **SI** | 13 | Electronics, silicon wafers, glass |
| **STL** | 13 | Construction, tools, ship parts |
| **TI** | 11 | High-tier hulls, ship parts, alloys |
| **CU** | 6 | Wiring, electronics, alloys |
| **LI** | 5 | Batteries (POW, CBL), medical |

**AL is the #1 metal in the game.** It's in almost everything. If you're running a smelter colony, prioritize AL throughput.

## Alloys (10)

Alloys combine two or more metals. Most are made in **ASM**, a few in **FS** (gold alloys).

### Advanced Smelter (ASM) Alloys

| Ticker | Name | Recipe | Time | Used For |
|--------|------|--------|------|----------|
| **FAL** | Ferro-Aluminum | 3× FE + 3× AL → 6× FAL | 9.6h | BGS, BWH, CQS, NOZ |
| **AST** | Aluminum-Titanium | 1× AL + 3× TI → 4× AST | 9.6h | AGS, ENG, HHP, ship engines |
| **FET** | Ferro-Titanium | 1× FE + 3× TI → 4× FET | 9.6h | AEN, AHP, ANZ, ship parts |
| **WAL** | Tungsten-Aluminum | 3× AL + 1× W → 4× WAL | 9.6h | CTF, FIR, HCB, HNZ, high-end ship parts |
| **ALR** | Aluminum-Rhenium | 5× RE + 1× AL → 6× ALR | 12h | TOR (torpedo hull) |
| **WRH** | Tungsten-Rhenium | 2× W + 4× RE → 6× WRH | 14.4h | SST (structural spacecraft tower) |

## Common Colony Patterns

### "SME Farm" (Pioneer only)

The lowest-barrier metal production. Drop 6–10 SMEs on an ore-rich planet with Pioneer-only workforce.

- **Pros:** Cheap to set up, no workforce upgrades needed
- **Cons:** Low margin, very common — prices often competitive
- **Tip:** Dedicate one planet to AL+FE+SI smelting since these three are the most demanded

### "ASM Colony" (Tech + Eng)

A more capital-intensive setup that produces alloys. Requires climbing to Technicians and Engineers.

- **Pros:** Higher margin, fewer competitors
- **Cons:** Complex supply chain (you need basic metals flowing in)
- **Tip:** Import basic metals rather than smelting on-site. An ASM colony with no SME is often more profitable than a mixed one.

### "Fine Smithy Niche" (Settler)

FS only makes 3 alloys (BRO/BGO/RGO) but all feed into high-value electronics. Low-volume, high-margin niche.

- **Tip:** BGO → PCB is a key production chain. If you can supply both halves, PCB sales are a steady earner.

## Tips

1. **FLX pays for itself on FE, AL, REO.** Don't skip it unless FLX is unusable (too expensive or no CHP available).
2. **STL needs C in enormous volume.** Each STL recipe consumes 8× O + carbon via FE. Plan O and C supply before scaling steel.
3. **AL is the single metal to over-produce.** Used in 41 downstream recipes. More AL capacity = more options.
4. **W (Tungsten) is the choke point for high-tier alloys.** WAL and WRH both need W, and W requires AML + rare mineral (BTS). Mid-game bottleneck.
5. **Gold alloys are PCB gold.** BGO→PCB is the most consumed electronic part in the game. If you find a cheap AU supply, invest in FS.
6. **Don't build ASM without steady metal imports.** Alloys have much more value density than raw metals — let a dedicated SME planet feed yours.
7. **Alloys are 9.6h standard.** ASM runs most recipes on a 9.6h cycle, making capacity planning simple: 1 ASM ≈ 2.5 recipes/day.
