# Ship Components

The deepest production chain in the game. A single ship requires **63 different components** across engines, hulls, kits, and shields. Many endgame engines sit **16+ production steps** from raw ores, and the rarest (HYR, VOE) take 5–7 days per unit. Understanding this chain is optional for most players — but if you want to sell ships or the parts to build them, this is the top of the pyramid.

## Tips

1. **Don't try to vertically integrate.** With 63 components across 10+ buildings, one company cannot efficiently produce all of them. Specialize in 2–3 categories.
2. **SSC is the universal ship product.** Every ship uses it for repairs. 2.4h cycle in WEL (Pioneer workforce) — start here if you want ship business with low entry.
3. **SKF is the simplest ship-chain building.** Kits all follow a similar pattern (metal + MFK), Settler/Tech workforce, moderate times. Good first ship factory.
4. **HAM is a contract item.** At 360h and 7 unit prefab inputs, it's rarely economical to build speculatively. Only make it on contract.
5. **WAI / SNM = Scientist gate.** Nav computers (NV1/NV2) are locked behind SL. Plan scientist housing before investing in ship electronics.
6. **VOE/HYR are 10-day+ cycles.** If you commit, expect a single ship factory to produce ~3 per month at maximum.
7. **ACS is the engine unlocker.** ACS needs WAI + SAR + FC + THF. Without ACS, you can't make ENG, AEN, FSE, FIR, RAG, or HTE. It's the choke point.
8. **KV (Kevlar textile) shows up in shields.** BRP and BWH use KV — from CLF (Clothing Factory). Don't forget the textile chain when planning shield production.
