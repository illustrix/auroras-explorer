# Production & Recipes

Production is the heart of Prosperous Universe. Every manufactured item is created by feeding specific inputs into a building and waiting for the recipe to complete.

## How Recipes Work

A recipe defines:

- **Building** — which building type runs this recipe (e.g., SME, FP, CHP)
- **Inputs** — materials consumed (e.g., 1× FEO)
- **Outputs** — materials produced (e.g., 1× FE)
- **Duration** — how long the production cycle takes

For example, smelting iron:

> **SME:** 1× FEO → 1× FE (4.8 hours)

## Production Chain Depth

Materials sit at different "depths" in the production chain:

- **Depth 0** — Raw materials (44 types): ores, gases, water, etc.
- **Depth 1–4** — Basic processed goods: metals, simple chemicals
- **Depth 5–7** — Intermediate products: electronics, construction parts (~150 types)
- **Depth 8–12** — Advanced products: ship components, software
- **Depth 13–16** — Endgame products: vortex engines, gateway segments

The deepest items (**GWS** and **VOE** at depth 16) require raw materials to pass through 16 sequential processing steps before becoming the final product.

## Food Production: RAT Recipes

One of the most important early-game products is **RAT (Rations)**. All RAT recipes follow a pattern:

> **Pick one from each of three groups → 10× RAT (6 hours)**

| Carbohydrate | Protein | Fat |
|-------------|---------|-----|
| GRN (Grains) | ALG (Algae) | NUT (Nuts) |
| MAI (Maize) | BEA (Beans) | VEG (Vegetables) |
| MUS (Mushrooms) | — | — |

The most economical combo uses only farm-grown ingredients: **MAI + BEA + NUT** — all produced in a basic Farm (FRM) with just water.

## Tips for Production Planning

1. **Balance your workforce** — Don't over-build one tier. Each building needs workers fed and housed.
2. **Consider transport costs** — It may be cheaper to buy intermediates from the CX than to produce them yourself.
3. **Watch the market** — Prices fluctuate. Today's profitable recipe might be tomorrow's money pit.
4. **Plan for uptime** — Buildings produce nothing when inputs run out. Keep buffers of critical materials.
